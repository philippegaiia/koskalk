## Anti-Patterns Verdict

LLM assessment: The page does not look careless, but it does have a recognizable AI landing-page skeleton: centered hero pill, oversized display serif headline, two CTAs, four benefit cards, two product cards, comparison table, workflow grid, founder quote, final CTA. The background image and bench-specific copy save it from full slop, but the composition is still more template-like than distinctive.

Deterministic scan: `impeccable detect --json resources/views/welcome.blade.php resources/views/layouts/public.blade.php` found 2 warnings:

- `pure-black-white` in `resources/views/layouts/public.blade.php:48`, caused by `bg-black/20` on the mobile overlay.
- `flat-type-hierarchy` in `resources/views/layouts/public.blade.php:17`, reading the shell as mostly 12px/14px/16px text.

Visual overlays: skipped. The installed CLI does not expose the documented `impeccable live` command in this package version, and the in-app browser evaluate context is read-only, so I could not inject `/detect.js` safely. I did inspect the live page visually at `http://koskalk.test/`.

## Overall Impression

The hero is the best part: it has a real product/bench signal, strong scale, and a clear promise. The rest of the page becomes too explanatory and too repetitive. Biggest opportunity: turn the homepage from "explaining a calculator/workspace" into "letting the visitor feel the calculator/workspace immediately."

## What's Working

- The above-the-fold promise is clear: quick calculation now, saved formula workspace later.
- The background image is on-topic and gives the page a real product surface instead of generic decoration.
- The two entry paths, free calculator versus saved workspace, are strategically correct.

## Priority Issues

### [P1] The homepage conflicts with the project design system

Why it matters: `DESIGN.md` says the system is "Clinical Naturalist", Instrument Sans, OKLCH tokens, restrained color, and no decorative borders. The homepage loads Lora/DM Sans/DM Mono in `resources/views/layouts/public.blade.php:11`, sets DM Sans inline at `:15`, and defines homepage-specific hex colors/fonts in `resources/css/shared/soapkraft.css:58-74`.

Fix: Either explicitly bless the homepage as a brand exception, or bring it back into the system: Instrument Sans, OKLCH tokens, fewer serif moments, less linework, and card depth by shadow/background shifts.

Suggested command: `/impeccable typeset homepage`

### [P1] The page tells instead of demonstrating

Why it matters: The primary CTA is "Use the free calculator", but the first viewport is a static marketing hero. Users looking for a lye calculator want confidence in seconds.

Fix: Add a compact, realistic calculator/formula preview in the first viewport or immediately below it: oils, superfat, NaOH/KOH/water result, and a "Save as formula" affordance. Make the product state visible before the benefits grid.

Suggested command: `/impeccable layout homepage`

### [P2] The CTA promise may create trust friction

Why it matters: "No account needed" and "Use the free calculator" point to `route('recipes.create')`, which renders as `/dashboard/recipes/new` in the DOM. Even if it works for guests, the URL and second CTA both feel account/workspace-shaped.

Fix: Make the calculator URL visibly public, or adjust the copy/route naming so the no-account promise and destination agree.

Suggested command: `/impeccable clarify homepage`

### [P2] Section structure leans on borders and grids against local rules

Why it matters: `DESIGN.md` says no 1px borders for sectioning. The homepage uses `border-y`, `gap-px bg-line`, `border`, `border-b`, and `border-t` heavily across `resources/views/welcome.blade.php:90-99`, `112-126`, `156-164`, and `184`.

Fix: Replace separators with surface shifts, spacing, and one card shadow level. Let grouping come from proximity and background, not outlines.

Suggested command: `/impeccable polish homepage`

### [P2] Trust proof needs more grounding

Why it matters: The founder quote and metrics (`1M+`, `16`, `2`) are memorable, but without labels or context they can read as decorative claims instead of evidence.

Fix: Add a small credibility note, make the metric labels more explicit, or replace the number block with a short "why I built this" bench story tied to real workflows.

Suggested command: `/impeccable clarify homepage`

## Cognitive Load

Failure count: 2/8, moderate.

The page has clear chunking and visible actions, but it repeats the same argument several times and introduces domain terms without help. Decision points stay under four options, which is good. Progressive disclosure is the weak spot: the page explains formula history, IFRA, label signals, costing, and private ingredients before showing enough of the product.

## Persona Red Flags

Jordan, first-timer: The first action is visible, but "lye", "KOH", "IFRA", and "label signals" appear without plain-language support. Jordan may understand that this is professional, but not what to click or what they'll get.

Riley, stress tester: The "no account needed" promise conflicts with the `/dashboard/recipes/new` URL shape. The metrics also invite skepticism without evidence.

Casey, distracted mobile user: The large hero and top navigation are likely heavy on small screens. The key action may sit above the thumb zone, and the background image could make the first load feel slower on mobile.

## Minor Observations

- The benefit cards are semantically all `h2` items after an `sr-only` Benefits heading. They should likely be `h3`.
- The mobile overlay uses `bg-black/20`, which the detector flagged. Use a tinted neutral overlay instead.
- The Laravel debugbar is visible in the browser screenshot. That is local-only, but it visually obscures the page while reviewing.

## Questions To Consider

- Should the homepage preserve the current brand-serif direction, or should it fully align to the Clinical Naturalist design system?
- Should the first viewport be a marketing story, or should it expose a compact calculator preview immediately?
- Is the top priority trust/clarity, or visual system consistency?
