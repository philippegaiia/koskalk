# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Soapkraft serves independent soapmakers, cosmetic makers, and small producers who need fast formulation help without losing the practical details that make a recipe reproducible. Some arrive for a free soap calculation and leave with a lye or water number. Others use the workspace as a working bench for saved formulas, private ingredients, costing, label signals, compliance preparation, notes, and production-ready records.

Users may be technically curious, craft-oriented, or commercially active, but they should not need to think like software operators. They need a tool that respects formulation work, keeps complexity close when it matters, and stays calm enough for repeated use.

## Product Purpose

Soapkraft turns quick soap and cosmetic formulation into a structured, reliable workspace. It starts with a low-friction calculator, then lets serious formulas become saved records with ingredients, phases, costs, label signals, compliance context, and revert-safe draft backups.

Success means users can calculate quickly, save only when it is worth saving, return to their formulas without confusion, and prepare practical formulation or compliance information without scattering it across spreadsheets, notebooks, screenshots, and disconnected tools.

## Brand Personality

Calm, precise, natural, and serious without becoming cold. The brand should feel like a thoughtful formulation bench: rigorous enough for chemistry and compliance, warm enough for makers, and restrained enough to be trusted.

The voice is direct and useful. It should explain, clarify, and reduce anxiety without becoming cute, salesy, or overconfident about regulatory decisions.

## Anti-references

Soapkraft should not look like an Etsy craft template, a generic SaaS card grid, a flashy AI-generated landing page, a neon dark lab interface, or a decorative handmade soap blog. Avoid gradient spectacle, playful craft motifs, glassy sci-fi panels, gratuitous icons, and anything that makes the product feel less serious than the formulas it stores.

Compliance should be present and clear, but not visually dominant for users who only need a calculator or recipe portfolio. The product should not imply that it replaces professional regulatory review where that review is required.

## Design Principles

1. Start fast, deepen only when needed.
2. Treat formulation as practical work, not decoration.
3. Keep chemistry, cost, label, and compliance signals near the formula, but preserve focus on the user's current task.
4. Make complexity legible through structure and hierarchy rather than visual noise.
5. Use saved drafts and backups as confidence tools, not as a heavy version-history product.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA for user-facing surfaces. Keyboard navigation, visible focus states, semantic landmarks, accessible form labels, readable contrast, and touch-friendly controls are baseline requirements.

Support reduced motion. Motion should clarify state changes or help orientation, never block completion. Mobile and tablet layouts must remain usable for both quick calculator use and dense workbench editing.
